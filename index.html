HF-02 — Transparency sheet
Opens on "Why am I seeing this?"
Design note
Feed control bar
Always visible below the tab bar. Chronological toggle (default off) gives full algorithm override. AI picks toggle (default on) makes recommendations explicit. Both are persistent — not buried in settings.
Design note
Content origin badges
Three badge types: friend (green), AI suggested (blue), Sponsored (amber). Every piece of content is labeled so users always know its origin.
Design note
Transparency chip
The "Why am I seeing this?" chip is always visible on every post — not hidden in a 3-dot menu. Tapping it opens the bottom sheet (HF-02) with a plain-language explanation of the ranking reason.
Design note
Override actions
"Move down" and "Not interested" are always visible in the action row. Actions are confirmed immediately. The AI learns from explicit signals rather than invisible behavioral tracking.

Controls visible · Content labeled
AI transparent · User in control
Key difference 1
Feed control bar (new)
The After screen adds a persistent control bar that was completely absent in the Before screen. This single addition gives users meaningful algorithmic control without any extra navigation.
Key difference 2
Content origin labeling (new)
Every post in the After screen carries a badge (friend, AI suggested, Sponsored). In the Before screen, there is no visual distinction between organic and algorithmically injected content.
Key difference 3
Transparency + override chips (new)
"Why am I seeing this?" and override actions appear on every post in the After screen. The Before screen has no equivalent — the algorithm's reasoning is permanently hidden from the user.
Metaphor shift
Agent → Supertool
Before: the algorithm decides everything, user has no visibility.

After: the algorithm assists, the user decides — which is what the product always claimed to be.
